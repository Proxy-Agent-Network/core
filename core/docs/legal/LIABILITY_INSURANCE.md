**Status:** Active (Mesa Pilot)

**Target:** Fleet Legal Counsel & Risk Management

## **1\. The Liability Bottleneck**

The primary friction point preventing Autonomous Vehicle (AV) fleets from decentralizing their physical recovery operations is risk. AV sensor arrays (LiDAR, Radar, Optical) are highly calibrated, expensive instruments, often exceeding $150,000 in hardware costs per vehicle.

Fleet Operators are understandably hesitant to allow unvetted third parties to physically interact with these sensors.

The Proxy Agent Network (PAN) mitigates this risk through a comprehensive, mathematically-bounded **$5,000,000 Liability Umbrella**. By dispatching a PAN Vanguard Agent, the Fleet Operator successfully transfers the risk of the physical intervention to our policy.

## **2\. The $5M Policy Structure**

PAN maintains a custom-underwritten commercial insurance policy specifically designed for autonomous infrastructure interventions. The policy consists of two primary pillars:

### **A. Hired Non-Owned Auto (HNOA) \- $2,000,000 Limit**

Because Vanguard Agents operate their own personal response vehicles to navigate to the stranded AV, PAN carries an HNOA policy. This shields the Fleet Operator from vicarious liability if a Vanguard Agent is involved in a traffic collision while *en route* to execute an active L402 contract.

### **B. Technology Errors & Omissions (Tech E\&O) \- $3,000,000 Limit**

This is the core coverage for the physical intervention. If a Vanguard Agent fails to follow the strictly mandated Optical Reclamation Protocol (ORP) and causes physical damage to the AV's sensor array (e.g., using an unauthorized abrasive material that scratches the LiDAR dome), this policy covers the hardware replacement and recalibration costs.

## **3\. Cryptographic Bounds of Liability**

Unlike traditional insurance which relies on vague timesheets, PAN's liability transfer is strictly defined by cryptographic timestamps generated by the Agent's mobile hardware (TPM 2.0 / Secure Enclave) and the AV's internal telemetry.

### **The Liability Window**

* **Assumption of Risk:** PAN's E\&O coverage initiates the exact millisecond the Vanguard Agent establishes the **UWB Proximity Lock** (\< 1.5 meters) and signs the presence attestation.  
* **Release of Risk:** PAN's E\&O coverage terminates the exact millisecond the AV broadcasts the **UDS FAULT\_CLEARED** webhook and releases the L402 payment.

*Legal Precedent:* By releasing the Lightning Network preimage to settle the bounty, the AV's onboard AI is legally asserting that the hardware is clean, functional, and undamaged. Any subsequent sensor failures that occur after the AV resumes autonomous routing are the sole liability of the Fleet Operator.

## **4\. Hardware Damage & Claim Adjudication**

In the rare event that an intervention results in hardware damage, the PAN platform ensures a frictionless adjudication process:

1. **Fleet Flagging:** The Fleet Operator flags the mission\_id via the PAN API within 24 hours of the intervention.  
2. **Audit Freeze:** The Vanguard Agent's account is temporarily suspended, and the SB 1417 Optical Health Report (including TPM signatures and GPS/UWB logs) is locked for review.  
3. **Forensic Review:** The Fleet Operator provides internal vehicle telemetry (and exterior camera footage, if available) showing the Agent utilizing unauthorized tools or kinetic force violating the "HP Potion" SOP.  
4. **Payout & Slashing:** If PAN Command verifies the SOP breach, the E\&O claim is processed, and the Vanguard Agent is permanently slashed from the network (HARDWARE\_DAMAGE Zero-Tolerance penalty).

## **5\. Exclusions & Indemnification**

PAN's liability shield **does not** cover:

* Internal software failures, compute node crashes, or EV battery thermal events. (Vanguard Agents are strictly prohibited from opening the vehicle's hood or accessing compute racks).  
* Incidents involving passengers inside the cabin, unless the Fleet Operator explicitly issued a CABIN\_EMERGENCY UDS override code.  
* Pre-existing physical damage to the vehicle (e.g., body damage from a prior collision that grounded the vehicle).

## **6\. Certificates of Insurance (COI)**

Upon executing a Master Service Agreement (MSA) with PAN, Fleet Partners are named as Additional Insureds on the primary HNOA and E\&O policies. Digital COIs are provided during the API credential provisioning phase.
