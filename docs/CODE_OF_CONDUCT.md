# Proxy Agent Network (PAN) | Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
We as members, contributors, Vanguard Agents, and PAN Command leaders pledge to make participation in our network a harassment-free and professional experience for everyone. This applies regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, military service background, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, disciplined, and mission-focused community.

---

## Our Standards (The Vanguard Ethos)
Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our infrastructure network include:

* **Prioritizing Safety:** Always defaulting to the physical safety of our Vanguard Agents and the integrity of Fleet Partner assets ($150k+ AVs).
* **Maintaining OPSEC:** Strictly adhering to Operational Security regarding Fleet telemetry, UDS fault codes, and passenger privacy.
* **Demonstrating Professionalism:** Acting as a disciplined representative of PAN when interacting with AV fleet engineers, local authorities (e.g., Mesa PD), and the public.
* **Respecting the Chain:** Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback regarding code commits, RFCs, and physical operational protocols (ORP).
* **Taking Ownership:** Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, whether in code or in the field.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* **OPSEC Violations:** Publishing Fleet Partner telemetry, vehicle locations, or passenger personally identifiable information (PII) without explicit, cryptographic authorization.
* **Physical Endangerment:** Proposing code changes or physical protocols that recklessly endanger Vanguard Agents or the public.
* **Unprofessional Conduct:** The use of sexualized language or imagery, trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, or political/personal attacks.
* **Harassment:** Public or private harassment of contributors, Agents, or enterprise partners.
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a highly regulated, enterprise infrastructure setting.

---

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all PAN digital spaces (GitHub, forums, API documentation) and physical Operational Design Domains (e.g., Mesa Sector 1). It also applies when an individual is officially representing PAN in public spaces, during fleet API integrations, or while actively deployed on a Machine-to-Human (M2H) recovery mission.

## Enforcement & Reporting
Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior—as well as severe OPSEC or safety violations—must be reported immediately to PAN Command at **security@proxyagent.network**. 

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly by the core team. PAN Command is obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident. We reserve the right to permanently revoke GitHub access, API keys, and Hardware Attestation (L402) credentials for any individual or Agent found in violation of these standards.
